To compensate for the loss of 4.37 ha of Eucalyptus ovata foraging habitat for the listed threatened species Swift Parrot (Lathamus discolour) at the development site, the person taking the action must provide a suitable offset package. The offset package must include the protection in perpetuity of an area of land (the protected land), which must:

contain at least 12 ha of E.ovata Forest and Woodland;
contain Swift Parrot foraging habitat values equal or greater than those being removed;
be located within the core eastern habitat for the Swift Parrot;
be located within the South East Bioregion.

The offset package must be approved by the Minister.4/11/2009033. The person taking the action must:

Within 6 months of the date of this approval, enter into an agreement with the owner of the protected land to secure the protected land by way of perpetual conservation covenant under the Nature Conservation Act 2002. The Department must be provided with a copy of the agreement within 2 weeks of its signature by all parties.
Within 12 months of the date of this approval, ensure that the conservation covenant for the protected land is executed and is registered on the title documents of the protected land. The Department must be provided with a copy of the signed conservation covenant within 2 weeks of its signature by all parties.4/11/2009044. Within 12 months of the date of this approval, a Nature Conservation Plan for the protected land, to remain valid for a minimum period of 10 years from the commencement date of the conservation covenant, must be completed by the person taking the action, endorsed by the land owner(s) and submitted to the Minister. The Nature Conservation Plan must:

Provide for the conservation and enhancement of Swift Parrot habitat on the protected land and include specific measures to:

maintain and enhance Swift Parrot habitat;
control weed species and pest animals;
control access to the protected land;
monitor the status of Swift Parrot populations and condition of Swift Parrot habitat; and
report the results of monitoring and management activities to the Department.


Include provisions and measures to ensure that actions taken to conserve, manage and protect Swift Parrot habitat have no detrimental impact on the habitat and populations of other EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities that are identified in the Nature Conservation Plan as occurring on the protected land.
Include corrective actions and contingency measures to be implemented where monitoring of the protected land under the Nature Conservation Plan indicates a degradation of Swift Parrot habitat.

The Nature Conservation Plan must be approved by the Minister.
