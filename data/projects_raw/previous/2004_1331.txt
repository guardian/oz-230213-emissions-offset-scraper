A management plan for the offset area must be prepared and submitted to the Victorian Department of Environment Primary Industries for approval by the end of June 2014. The approved plan must be submitted to the Department.31/10/201355. In order to minimise impacts on the Orange-bellied Parrot, the person taking the action must submit for the Minister's approval an Orange-bellied Parrot management plan (OBPMP). The plan must include, but not be limited to:

creation of an open space and wetlands area, as described in the Wyndham Harbour Landscape Concept Plan dated October 2009, and supplement dated July 2012;
reservation of the foreshore reserve, including retention of the 1 metre wide strip of Berm Grassy Shrubland between the Central Access Spine and the northern groyne in accordance with the Wyndham Harbour Landscape Concept Plan dated October 2009, and supplement dated July 2012;
revegetation and weed control of these areas to rehabilitate the Berm Grassy Shrubland to increase the quality and extent of habitat for the Orange-bellied Parrot using species identified in the coastal dune species list in the Wyndham Harbour Landscape Concept dated October 2009, and supplement dated July 2012.

The person taking the action must not commence construction unless the Minister has approved the OBPMP.
