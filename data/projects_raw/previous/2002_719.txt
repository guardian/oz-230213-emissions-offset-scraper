a minimum of 150ha, containing at least 80% of remnant vegetation of suitable quality for the Southern Cassowary (Casuarius casuarius johnsonii) as defined by the Queensland Vegetation Management Act 1999;
the land must be located such that its conservation significantly reduces one or both of the following identified threats to the Wet Tropics World Heritage Area:

fragmentation of external corridors that are important to maintaining the integrity of the Wet Tropics World Heritage Area, or
loss of habitat for species of conservation significance (i.e. listed species under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (Cwlth) and/or the Nature Conservation Act 1992 (Qld));


the land must provide some linkage between existing conservation reserves, and/or be adjacent to existing conservation reserves;
the land must either:

be protected by a legal instrument, attached to the title, that conserves the land for conservation purposes only for perpetuity, or
be in or be committed to be in a suitable conservation tenure (e.g. national park under the Nature Conservation Act 1992 (Qld)); and


a map delineating the extent of the conservation reserve and where it is in relation to 6(b) and 6(c).
