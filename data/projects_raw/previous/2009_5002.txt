3. The person taking the action must submit a Biodiversity Offset Strategy to the Minister for approval. The strategy must address the following requirements:

The acquisition and conservation of land containing a minimum of 110ha of habitat for the Square-fruited Ironbark that is equal or greater quality to that removed for the Pacific Highway Upgrade at Glenugie;
The acquisition and conservation of land containing a minimum of 70ha that will be revegetated to provide habitat for the Square-fruited Ironbark that is equal or greater quality to that removed for the Pacific Highway Upgrade at Glenugie;
Details of revegetation work required to achieve requirements of condition 3(b). The works must be consistent with advice from a suitably qualified expert;
The land referred to in condition 3(a) and 3(b) must provide linkage between existing stands of Square-fruited Ironbark and/or be adjacent to existing stands of Square-fruited Ironbark;
The land referred to in condition 3(a) and 3(b) must be located within 100km of the Pacific Highway Upgrade at Glenugie;
Research into the extent, population genetics and the success of regeneration of habitat for the Square-fruited Ironbark;
The acquisition and conservation of land containing a minimum of 120ha of habitat for the Large-eared Pied Bat, Grey-headed Flying Fox, Regent Honeyeater and Swift Parrot;
The land referred to in condition 3(g) must be located within 50km of Grafton and is in addition to that provided under condition 3(a);
The land referred to in condition 3(a), 3(b) and 3(g) must be protected by a legal instrument under relevant nature conservation legislation, that ensures the land is conserved in perpetuity;
The strategy must include commitments to ongoing management of the land referred to in condition 3(a), (b) and (g) in perpetuity;
The strategy must include key milestones, performance indicators, corrective actions and timeframes for the completion of all actions outlined in the strategy; and
The strategy must be developed in consultation with the Department.

The strategy must be submitted for approval by the Minister within eight months of the date of this approval. The strategy must satisfy the Minister.
