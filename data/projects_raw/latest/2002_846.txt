The person taking the action must submit to the Minister for approval, prior to construction, a Western Ringtail Management Plan (the Plan), addressing impacts of the action on the Western Ringtail Possum and its habitat. The Plan must include the following;

maps of the construction zone showing the area to be cleared and the location of temporary fencing required to protect the remaining native vegetation during construction activities;
measures to mitigate impacts on possums during vegetation clearing;
a program to monitor possum numbers in the whole of the Main Roads Reserve before and after clearing;
a contingency plan to translocate possums if the monitoring program above indicates that possum density has reached 15 or more per hectare within two weeks of clearing; and
a program to offset possum habitat loss by planting and maintaining a minimum of 200 Agonis flexuosa trees in a location that has been secured for long-term conservation protection.

Construction must not commence before the Western Ringtail Management Plan is approved.
