All Cycas megacarpa individuals proposed to be translocated from the construction right of way (CROW) and propagated must be replanted in the translocation site identified at Annexure 2 in accordance with condition 5.30/04/2010033. The translocation site identified at Annexure 2 must be managed for conservation and protected under local, state or federal nature conservation legislation to ensure that the land is conserved in perpetuity within 24 months from the date of this approval. Written evidence of compliance with this condition must be provided to the Department within 20 business days of finalisation of conservation arrangements.30/04/2010044. To ensure the establishment and viability of the translocated and propagated Cycas megacarpa population at Annexure 2, a population of no less then 600 individuals must be maintained for a minimum of 5 years after final planting and managed in accordance with condition 5.30/04/2010055. In order to protect the Cycas megacarpa, the person taking the action must prepare and submit a Cycas megacarpa Management Plan (CMMP) prior to the commencement of the action. The CMMP must be prepared in consultation with an expert agreed to by the Minister. The CMMP must include:

Measures to ensure all Cycas megacarpa within the CROW are translocated, or avoided using horizontal directional drilling (HDD);
Measures to propagate and plant Cycas megacarpa individuals removed or impacted by construction activities;
Detailed methodology for translocation, propagation and planting;
Details of a suitably qualified person to undertake translocation, propagation and planting;
Details of the erosion and sediment control measures to be implemented in the CROW in the Callide and Calliope Ranges;
Detailed measures to rehabilitate the CROW in the Callide and Calliope Ranges;
Details of the control and management of weeds in translocation site; and
Measures of the management, maintenance and protection of Cycas megacarpa individuals in the translocation site for a period of 5 years following the final planting.

The CMMP must be approved by the Minister prior to commencement of the action.
