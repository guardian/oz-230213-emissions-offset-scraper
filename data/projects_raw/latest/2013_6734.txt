3. The approval holder must prepare and submit an Offset Management Plan to the Minister for approval. The Offset Management Plan must include:

a detailed survey and description of the condition of Offset Area A shown at Appendix 2 prior to commencement of the action. The description must identify habitat suitable for Koala and vegetation which has the potential to be restored or improved;
offset attributes and maps in electronic Geographic Information System (GIS) format;
details of measures to be undertaken to improve Koala habitat within Offset Area B shown at Appendix 2 in line with the Nature Conservation (Koala) Conservation Plan 2006 and Management Program 2006-2016, including measures to ensure:

rehabilitation commences within 2 years of approval of the Offset Management Plan;
trees planted have been propagated from local seed stock, where possible;
regeneration with a similar composition of overstorey species (type, density and diversity) compared to the local variant of Regional Ecosystem (RE) 12.3.3 - Eucalyptus tereticornis woodland on Quaternary alluvium; and
revegetation is undertaken in consultation with the Queensland Department of Environment and Heritage Protection.


The Offset Management Plan must include:

details of the management, monitoring and reporting actions to be undertaken to ensure that regrowth vegetation identified as Koala habitat on Offset Area A at Appendix 2 is maintained for Koala conservation purposes;
details of the management, monitoring and reporting actions to be undertaken to ensure the rehabilitation of Offset Area B at Appendix 2; and
a rehabilitation schedule for Offset Area B outlining seed collection, propagation and planting, and management and monitoring of trees for 10 years after planting.



The Offset Management Plan must be submitted to the Minister for approval within 12 months of the commencement of the action.
