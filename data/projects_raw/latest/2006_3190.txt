Within three months of the approval being issued, the dedicated protected area referred to in Condition 2 must be fenced to prevent domestic and pest animals including rabbits and other burrowing animals from entering the site. Fencing must be constructed prior to any translocation of the Quassia bidwillii. Entry to the fenced area must be restricted to emergency vehicles and persons responsible for management of the dedicated protected area. Signs that describe this restricted access must be visibly and legibly positioned on the gate.12/06/200744. To protect the Quassia bidwillii plants, the person taking the action must commission a qualified botanist to conduct the following:

Revegetate the dedicated protected area referred to in Condition 2 with native vegetation identified in the Queensland Government Environment Protection Agency Regional Ecosystem 12.5.13 and suitable for planting with Quassia bidwillii.
Translocation of any individual Quassia bidwillii plants found on the site that are not already in the dedicated protected area. The translocation must be conducted in accordance with the latest edition of the Guidelines for the Translocation of Threatened Plants in Australia (translocation guidelines) by the Australian Network for Plant Conservation. In particular this should include:

the preparation and implementation of a translocation plan that includes, but is not limited to pre-translocation preparation, translocation methodology, and post-translocation monitoring schedule;
identification and recording of the number of translocated individuals, their baseline health prior to translocation, and their individual GPS locations once translocated;
monitoring of translocation success (in accordance with measures identified in the translocation guidelines) on a three monthly basis within the first year of translocation, and annually for each year thereafter for a period of ten years; and
prepare a report to the Department three months after the date of the approval, and annually thereafter for a period of ten years on translocation success as per the translocation guidelines;


prepare a plan of management outlining additional measures to establish and manage dedicated protected area as per Condition 4. This must include measures to conduct:

weed and pest control; and
irrigation for long-term revegetation and translocation success as per Conditions 4a and 4b.
