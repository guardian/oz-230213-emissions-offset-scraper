The approval holder must provide an offset of at least 72 hectares of habitat for black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) to compensate for the loss of 31.5 hectares of black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) habitat. The habitat quality of the offset must be at least equal to that of the impact site and contain black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) trees. The offset must achieve a conservation outcome that improves the viability of the black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) species.23/09/2016044. The offset strategy must include:

a map (including shapefiles) showing the location of the proposed offset/s;
details about the conservation values (including habitat quality, size and connectivity) of the offset in comparison to the black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) habitat being impacted; and
a summary of black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) habitat management actions and how those actions will improve the conservation benefits.23/09/2016055. The OMP must include:

the overall objectives of the plan;
a risk assessment;
the roles and responsibilities of persons responsible for actions under the OMP;
black ironbox (Eucalyptus raveretiana) habitat management actions and controls (summarised in the offset strategy at 4c);
the timing of actions;
a monitoring program, which must include:

baseline conditions;
performance indicators;
outcomes;
monitoring requirements; and
trigger values for corrective actions.


potential corrective actions for when trigger values are reached, and how environmental incidents and emergencies will be managed; and
auditing and review mechanisms.23/09/2016066. The approval holder must provide to the Department a summary report detailing what activities have been undertaken to implement the OMP, whether the OMP is meeting performance indicators or if any corrective actions are being taken. The first OMP summary report must be submitted within twelve months of the OMP being approved and then within every twelve months for the life of the approval.23/09/2016077.
