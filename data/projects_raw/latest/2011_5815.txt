The TSOP must include, but not necessarily be limited to:

a contribution of no less than $3,000,000 (GST exclusive) to fund, extend or expand a land management program within the Pilbara bioregion for a period of no less than five years;
details of measures to control and/or manage, for the benefit of the Northern Quoll and Pilbara Olive Python:

introduced predators;
feral herbivores;
wild fires; and
invasive weeds.


for each threat identified in condition 14) b), the TSOP must define:

how the control/management measures are expected to benefit the Northern Quoll and Pilbara Olive Python;
details of the location and area of land to be managed which must be mapped and provided to the department in a shapefile(s);
details of methodology, timing, frequency and intensity (effort) of management measures;
responsibility for management measures;
details of how the management actions identified for condition 14) b) will be undertaken in a manner that is sympathetic with the conservation of other relevant threatened species listed under the EPBC Act known to occur in the area identified under 14) c) ii);


details of a monitoring program, including but not necessarily limited to:

methodology, timing, frequency, scope and survey effort for/of monitoring;
baseline surveys of the area to be managed;
monitoring during and post land management actions to determine the effectiveness of land management actions;
performance indicators, which will determine the effectiveness of the land management program; and
measures to make the results of the monitoring made publically available.



The TSOP must be submitted to the Minister for approval by 31 December 2014.
