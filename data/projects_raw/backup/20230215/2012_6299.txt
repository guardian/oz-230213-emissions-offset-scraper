2. The person taking the action must submit a Biodiversity Offset Plan to the Minister for approval.

The Plan must consist of:

The undertaking of, or funding to undertake, activities that have the objective of long-term conservation gains for the Northern Quoll
The undertaking of, or funding to undertake, a diet analysis study of the Pilbara Olive Python, for the better protection and long term conservation of the species
The undertaking of, or funding to undertake, an ecological and seed biology study (seed longevity, soil seed storage, establishment requirements, provenance zones) of the Hamersley Lepidium, including supporting the long-term storage of seeds from across populations for the better protection and conservation of the species


The Plan must include the following details as a minimum:

Descriptions of proposed offset activities, as well as details of who will undertake the activities and the costs of undertaking the activities
Methods, key milestones, performance indicators and timeframes for the completion of all offset activities.



The Offset Plan must be developed in consultation with the WA DEC, and must be approved by the Minister within 12 months of commencement of the action, or a period otherwise agreed to by the Minister.
