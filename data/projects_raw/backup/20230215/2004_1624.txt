Revegetate the parks with native vegetation consistent with that identified in the Queensland Government Environment Protection Agency Regional Ecosystems 12.5.13, 12.5.4 or 12.3.11 suitable for planting with Quassia bidwillii. b. Translocate of any individual Quassia bidwillii plants found on the site that are not already in the parks. The translocation must be conducted in accordance with the latest edition of the Guidelines for the Translocation of Threatened Plants in Australia (translocation guidelines) by the Australian Network for Plant Conservation. In particular this should include: i. the preparation and implementation of a translocation plan that includes, but is not limited to pre-translocation preparation, translocation methodology, and post-translocation monitoring schedule; ii. identification and recording of the number of translocated individuals, their baseline health prior to translocation, and their individual GPS locations once translocated; iii. monitoring of translocation success (in accordance with measures identified in the translocation guidelines) on a three monthly basis within the first year of translocation, and annually for each year thereafter for a period of ten years; and iv. prepare a report three months after the date of the approval, and annually thereafter for a period of ten years on translocation success as per the translocation guidelines; c. prepare a plan of management outlining additional measures to establish and manage the parks as per Condition 3.
