The person taking the action must establish a Nature Refuge Agreement under the Nature Conservation Act 1994 (Queensland) to protect an area of 712ha, as described in section 3.10.5.5 of the East Pit Project Environmental Management Plan dated May 2008; and to monitor the area as described in section 3.10.5.13 of the East Pit Project Environmental Management Plan.
Evidence of the establishment of the Nature Refuge Agreement must be provided to the Department by 30 April 2010.
The person taking the action must not remove any areas of Brigalow (Acacia harpophylla dominant and co-dominant) ecological community in the footprint of the proposed action until the Department is satisfied that the Nature Refuge Agreement has been established.8/10/200922. The person taking the action must notify the Minister in writing of any intention to alter the status of the Nature Refuge Agreement as referred to in Condition 1 and outline a strategy to provide for the conservation of an at least equivalent existing area if the Nature Refuge Agreement is to be removed.18/07/200833. The person taking the action must submit, for the Ministers approval, a plan to offset the loss of 44.2ha of Brigalow (Acacia harpophylla dominant and co-dominant) ecological community and to manage this community. The plan must include:

a map detailing the location of the offset, its associated vegetation types, habitat values and infrastructure;
measures for the long-term protection of at least an existing area of 80ha of remnant Brigalow (Acacia harpophylla dominant and co-dominant) ecological community;
measures to survey and monitor the occurrence of flora and fauna species including but not limited to the Squatter Pigeon (Geophaps scripta scripta) and the Ornamental Snake (Denisonia maculata);
measures for weed and feral animal control, fire management, erosion and sediment control, exclusion of livestock, and restrictions on access;
the development and implementation of a monitoring program including performance criteria against which the effectiveness of this plan can be measured; and
a process to review and report on this plan and on any unplanned events which may impact the offset.

The approved plan must be implemented.
