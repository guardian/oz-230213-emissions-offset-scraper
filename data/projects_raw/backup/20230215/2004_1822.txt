Prior to the clearing of remnant Brigalow vegetation associated with the Ensham Central Project, the person taking the action must submit for the Ministers approval a management plan (Nogoa River Conservation Plan) for the area of remnant vegetation and regrowth with a brigalow component adjacent to the Nogoa River. The Nogoa River Conservation Plan must include:
(a)     a map detailing the location of the conservation area, its associated vegetation types, habitat values and infrastructure;
(b)     measures for the long-term protection of the conservation area;
(c)     measures to monitor the occurrence of flora and fauna species including but not limited to the component species of Brigalow (RE 11.3.1), Brigalow Scaly-foot (Paradelma orientalis), Ornamental Snake (Denisonia maculata), Southern Squatter Pigeon (Geophaps scripta scripta), Yakka Skink (Egernia rugosa) and Dunmalls Snake (Furina dunmalli);
(d)     measures for weed and feral animal control, fire management, erosion and sediment control, monitoring of subsidance impacts, exclusion of livestock and restrictions on access;
(e)     the development and implementation of a monitoring program including performance criteria against which the effectiveness of this plan can be measured; and
(f)     a process to review and report on this plan and on any unplanned events which may impact the integrity of the area.
The clearing of remnant Brigalow vegetation may not commence until the plan has been approved. The approved plan must be implemented within 5 years of the date of this approval.
