The person taking the action must prepare an Offset Management Plan to offset the loss of habitat for the listed threatened and migratory Red-tailed Black-Cockatoo (Calyptorhynchus banksii graptogyne) as a result of clearing in areas to be mined.
This Offset Management Plan must include:

The purpose of the Plan, including desired outcomes of implementing the Plan;
The short (12 months from the date of the approval), medium (within three years of the date of the approval) and long term (within five years from the date of the approval) measures that would be employed to implement the Offset Management Plan;
Details of how the person taking the action will provide for the long term security of the offset areas;
Detailed performance and completion criteria for the implementation of the Offset Management Plan;
A detailed description of how the performance of the implementation of the Offset Management Plan would be monitored over time to achieve the objectives and completion criteria;
A description of the potential risks to successful rehabilitation in the offset area, and a description of the contingency measures that would be implemented to mitigate these risks; and
Details of who is responsible for monitoring, reviewing and implementing the Plan.

The Offset Management Plan must be submitted to the Minister for approval. The action cannot commence until the Offset Management Plan is approved by the Minister.
