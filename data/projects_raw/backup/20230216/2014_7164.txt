To offset the loss of habitat for listed Black Cockatoo species, within 18 months of the date of this approval, the person taking the action must provide written evidence to the Department that a legally binding Conservation Covenant has been registered over the offset area identified at Figure 1. The Conservation Covenant must be secured in perpetuity and must provide for the protection of no less than 35 ha of listed Black Cockatoo species foraging habitat.19/06/2014033. To offset the loss of habitat for listed Black Cockatoo species, the person taking the action must prepare and submit an Offset Plan for the Minister's written approval by 30 April 2020. If the Minister approves the Offset Plan, the plan must be implemented. The Offset Plan must be in effect for the duration of the approval and include, but not be limited to:


A textual description and map clearly defining the location and boundaries of the offset area, as specified in this condition, which must be accompanied with the offset attributes and shapefile;


A survey program to collect comprehensive baseline data on habitat quality for listed Black Cockatoo species, including vegetation condition and weed infestations

Milestones and objectives;
A monitoring program, that includes:


measurable performance indicators


the timing and frequency of monitoring to detect changes in the performance indicators;


trigger values for corrective actions; and


proposed corrective actions, if trigger values are reached.



Details of management measures for the offset area including measures to exclude weeds, third party access, intrusion of agricultural activities/practices and feral animals from the offset area;

Roles and responsibilities of personnel associated with implementing each of the above measures;


The Offset Plan must be prepared by a suitably qualified person;

The Offset Plan must be consistent with the principles of the EPBC Act Environmental Offsets Policy and with the Department’s Environmental Management Plan Guidelines.
