To compensate for the loss of Orange-bellied Parrot habitat, the person taking the action must protect at least 2 ha of land as shown at Appendix 1.12/11/20181010. The person taking the action must demonstrate that the offset site is protected in perpetuity (for example, through a Trust for Nature covenant) within six months of the date of this notice being signed.12/11/20181111. At least three months prior to commencement of construction, the person taking the action must prepare and submit to the Minister for approval, an Offset Management Plan. The person taking the action must not commence construction until the Offset Management Plan has been approved by the Minister and the person taking the action has commenced implementing the Offset Management Plan. At a minimum, the Offset Management Plan must include:

a copy of any contractual arrangement made by the Lonsdale Golf Course Inc. for the ongoing management of the offset site;
base line data and other supporting evidence that documents the extent and condition of foraging habitat for the Orange-bellied Parrot at the offset site;
illustrations and maps that clearly define the location and boundaries of the offset site. This must be accompanied with the offset attributes and a shapefile for the offset site;
detailed information, including proposed commitments and timelines regarding management arrangements that will be undertaken at the offset site, starting prior to commencement of construction and continuing for at least the life of the approval, to ensure the ongoing rehabilitation and improvement of the offset site. This must be consistent with all recommended habitat management measures identified in relevant EPBC Act policy statements and papers, and the National Recovery Plan for the Orange-bellied Parrot (Neophema chrysogaster);
completion criteria and performance targets for evaluating the effectiveness of Offset Area Management Plan implementation, and criteria for triggering corrective actions (if necessary);
information and commitments about monitoring and reporting on the improvements in habitat condition of the offset site and the ongoing use of the site by Orange-bellied Parrots;
