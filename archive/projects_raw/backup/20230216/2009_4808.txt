To compensate for the loss of critically endangered Gippsland Red Gum (Eucalyptus tereticornis subsp. mediana) Grassy Woodland and Associated Native Grassland Ecological Community at the Traralgon East (refer to Attachment 1, east of Leesons Road, west of Stammers Road) industrial subdivision development site, the person taking the action must ensure that 20.3 hectares of private land on property Lot 25 Briagolong Road, Briagolong, Victoria ('the protected land', refer to Attachment 3) containing Gippsland Red Gum (E. tereticornis subsp. mediana) Grassy Woodland and Associated Native Grassland Ecological Community, is protected in perpetuity.
The person taking the action must:

Within 3 months of the date of this approval, secure the tenure of the protected land by co-signing with Trust for Nature and the protected land owner a Credit Trading Agreement. The Department must be provided with a copy of the signed Credit Trading Agreement within 2 weeks of its signature by all parties.
Within 6 months of the date of this approval, ensure that either a Deed of Covenant or a section 173 agreement under the Victorian Planning and Environment Act 1987 for the protected land which provides for native vegetation protection is executed with Trust for Nature and is registered on the title documents of the protected land. The Department must be provided with a copy of the signed Deed of Covenant or section 173 agreement within 2 weeks of its signature by all parties.
Fund the implementation of the offset plan titled Offset Plan Lot 25 Briagolong Road, Briagolong for Princes Highway, Traralgon East (Lot B PS537834 D Lot 3 PS445357) Latrobe City Permit no. : 2008/35 prepared by Trust for Nature October 2009, for a period of 10 years from the date of this approval.
