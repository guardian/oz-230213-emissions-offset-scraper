Stage 1

To minimise impacts on the Western Ringtail Possum (Pseudocheirus occidentalis) the person taking the action must implement the following measures for Stage 1 in accordance with the Development Guide Plan dated 10 June 2008 at Attachment 1:

Conservation of the 0.81 ha remnant Peppermint patch in the north-east corner as public open space.
Establishment of minimum 40 metre width migratory corridors throughout the site, Living Stream reserve across the centre of the site and one narrow width (approximately 3-5 metre) migratory corridor along the north eastern side boundary of the site.
Planting of Peppermint-dominant native plant community and understorey in migratory corridors and Living Stream reserve as described in 1 (b) above.
Submission to the Department for approval prior to revegetation within Stage 1, a detailed Landscape Management Plan for replanting within Stage 1 that ensures a survival rate of at least 70 of replanted material. The approved Landscape management Plan must be implemented.
Existing vegetation and revegetation which provides habitat for the Western Ringtail Possum in any Public Open areas or proposed corridors referred to in (a) and (b) must not be removed.



Stage 2 
1.1 To minimise impacts on the Western Ringtail Possum (Pseudocheirus occidentalis) the person taking the action must implement the following measures for Stage 2:
a. Submission to the Department for approval, prior to the commencement of any construction within Stage 2, a detailed landscape Management Plan for replanting within Stage 2 that ensures a survival rate of at least 70 of replanted material. The Landscape Management Plan must include but not be limited to the following:
i. Development Guide Plan for stage 2; and
ii. Planting of an area no less than nominally indicated in the Development Guide Plan dated 10 June 2008 at Attachment 1.
b. Construction within Stage 2 must not commence until the Department has approved the Landscape Management Plan for stage 2.
