The principal and overriding objective of the Biodiversity Management Plan must be to manage and preserve the environmental reserve for conservation in perpetuity. The natural attributes of the environmental reserve such as, but not limited to, natural areas, habitat, wildlife corridors, vegetation and landscape features are to be preserved.
The Biodiversity Management Plan must address the following matters:

The transfer of the 530ha of the land shown in Map 1 to a single management authority;
Funding arrangements for the management of the reserve;
Provide a map delineating the surveyed environmental reserve boundaries as well as the placement of infrastructure and zones for the management of listed species;
Management methods for the areas identified in paragraph 2)c). This must include the identification of areas to be rehabilitated, and methods of rehabilitation;
Outline mitigation measures to be implemented within the environmental reserve to protect listed threatened and migratory species;
Public access and visitor facilities such as car parking, boardwalks, picnic facilities, fishing access, educational facilities and other such activities; and
Timeframes and reporting on the completion of matters required under paragraphs 2)a), 2)c) and 2)d).

Construction cannot commence until the Biodiversity Management Plan is approved.
