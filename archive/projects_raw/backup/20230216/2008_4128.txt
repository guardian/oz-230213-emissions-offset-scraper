Measures to enhance habitat for the Southern Bent-wing Bat including: 

The rehabilitation of a Southern Bent-wing Bat roosting cave (depending on location and issues associated with its closure); or 
The revegetation of approximately 15 ha of cleared land in the event that the Mortlake East and South site proceed, or in the event that only Mortlake South wind farm proceeds, the revegetation of approximately 8 ha of cleared land. The area to be revegetated must provide a habitat linkage to an area of existing vegetation. Area identified must be protected under an existing covenant.


In the event that any turbines are constructed in the area within the Mortlake East site extent, measures must be provided in the Habitat Enhancement Plan which will rehabilitate area(s) of habitat for listed migratory species. The Habitat Enhancement Plan must include but not be limited to the following measures:

the identification of a degraded wetland for rehabilitation;
information on the suitability of the site as habitat for migratory bird species;
the identification and planting of suitable wetland flora species;
measures to protect sensitive areas from the impacts of grazing and other threats;
measures to control weeds;
identification of the land tenure and long-term management of the site.


The Habitat Enhancement Plan must be approved by the Minister prior to construction (excluding the Tapps Lane access road and site compound).
