To compensate for the loss of 2.99ha of Swamp Gum (Eucalyptus ovata) foraging habitat for the endangered Swift Parrot (Lathamus discolor) from the site of the approved action, the area of Swift Parrot habitat identified in either Figure 1 (23.3ha) or Figure 2 (19ha) must be protected in perpetuity.16/07/2010022. The person taking the action must, within 3 months of this variation notice, ensure that a perpetual conservation covenant for land referred to in condition 1 is executed and is registered on the title documents of the land. The Department must be provided with a copy of the signed conservation covenant with 2 weeks of its signature by all parties.10/04/2012033. The offsets referred to in condition 1 must be managed in accordance with a Nature Conservation Plan. The Nature Conservation Plan must be provided to the Minister for approval within 3 months of this variation notice. The approved Nature Conservation Plan must be implemented for a period of at least 10 years from its approval. The Nature Conservation Plan must provide for the conservation and enhancement of the Swift Parrot habitat on the protected land, and:

Include specific measures to:

control weed species and pest animals;
control access to the protected land;
monitor on an annual basis the presence or absence of Swift Parrots and condition of Swift Parrot habitat, and note any nesting or breeding activity observed;
report the results of monitoring and management activities to the Department; and
identify other EPBC ACT listed threatened species and ecological communities that occur within the proposed offset areas.


Include measures to ensure that actions taken to conserve and enhance Swift Parrot habitat have no detrimental impact on the habitat and populations of other EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities that are identified in the Nature Conservation Plan as occurring on protected land.
