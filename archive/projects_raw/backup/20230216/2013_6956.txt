To compensate for the loss of box gum woodland and superb parrot habitat within the project site, within 12 months from the date of this approval, the approval holder must legally secure the offset site for conservation purposes as further prescribed in Condition 10.13/05/20141010. For the enduring conservation, management and monitoring of the offset site, the approval holder must engage a suitably qualified person to develop a Biodiversity Offset Management Action Plan (BOMAP). The BOMAP must be submitted to the Minister within 6 months from the date of this approval. The BOMAP must include details on:

location of the offset site including clear maps and accompanying shapefiles
historical, current and intended future ecological values of the offset site, with reference to its claimed offset scores
management activities including:

management of grazing and other human disturbances (including erection of any fencing to control stock)
management of weeds and pathogens (including environmental weed control via herbicide spray and targeted reduction cover)
management of pests and feral animals
management of fire (in consideration of ecological requirements)
retention of regrowth and remnant native vegetation
supplementary planting of native species via direct seeding / revegetation
erosion control
retention of dead timber and rocks


objectives and performance measures in relation to management activities included in c) (these should make reference where relevant to the claimed offset scores), along with corresponding contingency or remedial measures
scheduling and costing estimates for activities described in c).
