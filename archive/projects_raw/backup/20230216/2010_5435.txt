Within 12 months of commencement of operations the approval holder must submit for the approval of the Minister, an Offset Management Plan. The Offset Management Plan must be prepared in accordance with the EPBC Act Environmental Offsets Policy and Environmental Management Plan Guidelines and include:

confirmation of the legally binding conservation mechanism for the protection of the offset area(s);
a description of the proposed management measures and rehabilitation activities to be implemented on the offset area(s);
performance and completion criteria for evaluating the management of the offset area(s), and criteria for triggering remedial action (if necessary);
details of the timelines to monitor and report on the effectiveness of management measures and rehabilitation activities, and progress against performance and completion criteria, and to detect triggers for remedial action; and
a description of potential risks to the successful implementation of the plan, and a description of the contingency measures that will be implemented to mitigate against these risks, including a commitment to secure an additional offset area(s) if:

Malleefowl are not present on the offset area(s) by 5 years after the approval of the Fauna Offsets Strategy; or
final rehabilitation performance criteria specified in the Offset Management Plan are not met by 10 years after the approval of the Fauna Offsets Strategy.
