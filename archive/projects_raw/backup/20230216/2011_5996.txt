To offset the impact to foraging and potential breeding habitat of the Carnaby's Black Cockatoo, Baudin's Black Cockatoo and Forest Red-tailed Black Cockatoo, the person taking the action must undertake the action in accordance with the Runnymede Rd Sand mine: Black Cockatoo Management Plan (BCMP) dated August 2011, including implementing the following:

Within 12 months of the date of this notice, the person taking the action must revegetate 2.28 hectares with suitable Black Cockatoo habitat species within the area identified on the map at Appendix B as the 'revegetation' area;
The person taking the action must ensure that no less than 7.03 hectares of native vegetation is retained in perpetuity, and protected and managed in accordance with the BCMP, within the area identified on the map at Appendix B as the 'buffer' area.
Within 12 months of completion of mining operations, the person taking the action must revegetate a further 2.61 hectares with suitable Black Cockatoo habitat species within the area identified on the map at Appendix B as the 'additional revegetation offset' area.
Monitoring of seedling survival must be undertaken biannually in the revegetated/rehabilitated areas (as required under conditions 2a and 2c), until such time as planting is completed. Accurate records substantiating seedling survival must be maintained and made available upon request to the Department (as specified under condition 7).
