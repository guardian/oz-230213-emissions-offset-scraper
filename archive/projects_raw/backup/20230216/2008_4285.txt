The person to whom the approval is granted must submit to the Minister for approval a Biodiversity Offset Strategy to ensure the better protection of EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities. Commencement of construction of the approved action must not occur until the Biodiversity Offset Strategy has been approved by the Minister. The Biodiversity Offset Strategy must contain, but is not limited to, the following:

Details of the acquisition and ongoing management of no less than 27 ha of vegetation which meets the definition of the endangered ecological community Brigalow (Acacia harpophylla dominant and co-dominant), and includes habitat for the Brigalow Scaly-foot (Paradelma orientalis) and Squatter Pigeon (southern) (Geophaps scripta scripta);
Details of the acquisition and ongoing management of no less than five Boggomoss Springs located within 10 km of the Glebe Weir expansion;
Details of steps to be undertaken to secure in perpetuity the offset areas identified in conditions 3a and 3b of this approval. Offset areas must be secured in perpetuity using a Nature Refuge Agreement under the Queensland Nature Conservation Act 1994 or similar, must be on title, must be contiguous (but not as regards the Boggomoss Springs), and must not be located within an existing mining lease or mining lease application area; and
Details of the offset areas including maps (and accompanying shapefile) and site description, environmental values relevant to EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities, connectivity with other habitats and biodiversity corridors, and mechanisms for long-term protection, conservation and management.5/02/2013044. Within six months (6) of the approval of the Biodiversity Offset Strategy by the Minister, referred to in approval condition 3, the person to whom the approval is granted must submit to the Minister for approval an Offset Management Plan. The Offset Management Plan must detail how the offset areas identified in the Biodiversity Offset Strategy will be managed for the long-term protection and conservation of EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities. The Offset Management Plan must contain, but is not limited to, the following:

Measures to survey and monitor the occurrence of flora and fauna species including, but not limited to, Brigalow Scaly-foot (Paradelma orientalis) and Squatter Pigeon (southern) (Geophaps scripta scripta);
Details of objectives and targets for the conservation, protection and enhancement of the biodiversity values of each offset site for EPBC Act listed threatened species and ecological communities;
Measures for the long-term management and conservation of the proposed offset areas, including but not limited to, the exclusion of livestock, restriction of access, weed and feral animal control, fire management, erosion and sediment control; and
Key milestones, performance indicators, corrective actions, timeframes and responsibilities for the completion of the actions outlined in the Offset Management Plan.

The person taking the action must not impact or remove any EPBC Act listed species or ecological communities until the Offset Management Plan is approved.
