5. The approval holder must provide $100 000 per annum for 10 years to the WA DEC from the date of this decision, as a contribution to a trust fund agreed to by the department, for the better protection and long term conservation of EPBC Act listed threatened fauna species in the Pilbara region of Western Australia. The trust fund will coordinate research on EPBC Act listed threatened fauna, that focuses the following matters amongst others: a) lifecycles b) threatening processes; c) identification of habitat requirements and important populations; and d) long term conservation requirements to ensure persistence of the species.
Within 6 months of commencement of construction the approval holder must enter into an agreement with the WA DEC for the purposes of Northern Quoll Pilbara population research. The agreement must include:

the aims of the Northern Quoll Pilbara population research;
timeframes for fieldwork; and
timeframes for compilation of fieldwork results.

The first payment must be provided within 6 months of commencement of construction and subsequent payments made on that date annually.
