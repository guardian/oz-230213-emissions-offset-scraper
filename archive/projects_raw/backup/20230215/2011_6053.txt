The Offset Strategy must be consistent with the EPBC Act Environmental Offsets Policy, and must:

include the details of proposed offset area(s) containing native vegetation of the same, or better, quality of Carnaby’s Black Cockatoo foraging habitat as the native vegetation and pine planatation in the project area, located within 20 kilometres of the project area and totalling 1600 hectares¹ in area, or another area as agreed in writing by the Minister.

provide justification to demonstrate that the offset area(s) is consistent with the principles of the EPBC Act Environmental Offsets Policy.
detail the process that must be undertaken to ensure the offset area(s) is incorporated within conservation estate under management by the Western Australia Department of Biodiversity, Conservation and Attractions.

The approval holder must publish the approved Offset Strategy on the website within 10 business days of it being approved by the Minister and keep it published on the website until at least five years after the commencement of the action. The approval holder must commence implementing the approved Offset Strategy prior to commencement of the action. The approval holder must purchase and cede the offset area(s) to the Western Australia Department of Biodiversity, Conservation and Attractions for its protection in perpetuity within two years of commencement of the action, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Minister.
