The person taking the action must submit a Biodiversity Offset Strategy for the White box-Yellow Box- Blakely's Red Gum Grassy Woodland and Derived Native Grasslands to the Minister for approval. The strategy must address the following requirements:

Evidence of an arrangement to lease and enter into a Land Management Agreement under the ACT Land (Planning and Environment) Act 1991 for the permanent protection of ecological communities in the area indicated as Area 2 in Appendix A.
Measures to protect and enhance the biodiversity of Area 2 at Appendix A; in particular the ecological community White box-Yellow box-Blakely's Red Gum Grassy Woodland and Derived Native Grasslands. The measures must be consistent with the Land Management Agreement in condition 1 a) and include but not be limited to:

Fencing to prevent unwanted stock entry;
Retention of all native trees and saplings;
A stocking regime that will protect and enhance Box Gum Woodland; and
Regular weed monitoring and removal.


Measures to manage vegetation along the central sub-transmission line easement at Appendix B, including:

The removal of a maximum of 70 trees which are part of the White box-Yellow box- Blakely's Red Gum Grassy Woodland and Derived Native Grasslands ecological community;
Rehabilitation of all disturbed areas at the conclusion of the construction;
Clearing and slashing regimes of vegetation within the easement that will maintain and enhance the understorey component of White box-Yellow Box-Blakely's Red Gum Grassy Woodland and Derived Native Grasslands;
Confirmation of the total area cleared and trees removed;
Maintain erosion and sedimentation measures following construction for a period of 12 months; and
Details of weed management program.



The strategy must be submitted for approval to the Minister within six (6) months of the date of this approval.
