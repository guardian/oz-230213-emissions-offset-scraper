No. . sort ascendingDetails. sort descendingStart Date. sort descending066. Protected Areas Report for the Greater Bilby in the Pilbara Bioregion
To offset the impacts of the action to the Greater Bilby and contribute to the ongoing protection and long term conservation of this species in the Pilbara Bioregion, the person taking the action must fund the preparation of a Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report. Prior to funding the report and within three months of the date of this approval the person taking the action must provide to the department, the following information:

Details of a suitably qualified independent expert (including qualifications and experience) who is nominated to prepare the Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report;
Proposed time frames for the preparation and submission of the Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report to the department; and
The scope of issues to be addressed within the Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report.

The Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report must address (but need not be limited to) the following criteria:

Identify and map land systems and vegetation types that provide habitat for the Greater Bilby in the Pilbara Bioregion and, identify the land tenure of these areas;
Assess the status of the Greater Bilby in the Pilbara Bioregion;
Identify and map the protected area network in the Pilbara Bioregion, including national parks, proposed conservation reserves and private conservation reserves;
Assess the protected areas network in the Pilbara Bioregion in terms of its ability to provide long term protection for important populations and critical habitat of the Greater Bilby;
Present any information that is available to suggest that the Greater Bilby is present in any conservation reserve within the Pilbara Bioregion;
Provide a discussion of potential measures to address any deficiencies identified in the analysis (described in iv)), including the identification of land that affords critical habitat for the Greater Bilby but is currently unprotected or under threat; and
Provide a discussionon the potential for climate change to impact protected habitat areas for the Greater Bilby.

The Greater Bilby Protected Areas Report must be submitted to the department within one month of its completion.
