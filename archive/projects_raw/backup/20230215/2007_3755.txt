To compensate for the loss of listed threatened species, specifically a Golden Sun Moth population, the person taking the action must prepare and offer a package of compensatory measures. The package may include a range of measures to promote conservation and recovery of the species, but must consist of, at least in part:

the permanent reservation and management of land within the Victoria ( but outside the Western Grasslands Reserve) containing known Golden Sun Moth populations of medium to high density and of a sufficient area to provide reasonable confidence that the populations will persist on the site in the long-term; and
a program of surveys of likely habitat within the Victorian Volcanic Plain including, but not limited to, the shires / cities of Hume and Whittlesea for Golden Sun Moth during the 2009-10 flight season.
