1. To protect threatened species listed under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 (the EPBC Act), in particular the Matted Flax-Lily (Dianella amoena) the person taking the action must:

retain, protect and manage in perpetuity the two conservation reserves of 2.544 hectares and 2.743 hectares, known respectively as 400 McDonalds Road and 485 McDonalds Road, Mill Park, Victoria and identified in Attachment A to this notice;
implement the Plenty Valley Town Centre: Environmental Management Plan for the two reserves located at 400 and 485 McDonalds Road, Mill Park, Victoria, June 2009 (the Environmental Management Plan), including meeting all monitoring and reporting requirements in accordance with the Environmental Management Plan for a period of 10 years after the establishment of the conservation reserves. If monitoring indicates a decrease in the population of D. amoena, corrective actions and contingency measures must be developed and implemented, and a report on actions / measures provided to the Department two months after monitoring results are known;
translocate all D. amoena plants that occur within the development footprint of the proposed action (but outside the conservation reserves) into the conservation reserves in accordance with the 485 McDonalds Road, Mill Park, Victoria; Translocation Plan for the endangered Matted Flax-lily, March 2008, and the addendum to it dated 26 June 2009 (together, the Translocation Plan). Salvage and translocation must be undertaken by a contractor with qualifications and / or experience in the successful salvage and translocation of D. amoena. Salvage and translocation must be completed by 30 December 2010; and
prior to construction, provide the Minister with a copy of the written agreement with a contractor "suitably qualified" to manage the conservation reserves in accordance with the Environmental Management Plan for a minimum of 10 years.7/08/20092Land tenure
