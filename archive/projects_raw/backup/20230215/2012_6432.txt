To compensate for the loss of approximately 886 clumps of Black-Eyed Susan (Tetratheca juncea) and 195 Small-Flower Grevillea (Grevillea parviflora subsp. parviflora) plants, the person taking the action must protect and manage for conservation the biodiversity offset area identified in Schedule 1 of this notice, which is within the greater Awaba Biodiversity Conservation Area. The biodiversity offset area must be protected by a secure and enduring form of conservation covenant, the form of which must be a Positive Public Covenant made under s.88D of the Conveyancing Act 1919 (NSW). Prior to commencement of the action, the person taking the action must register the Positive Public Covenanton the land title. Once the covenant is registered, its provisions must be implemented.
The person taking the action must notify the department in writing at least two months prior to making any application to vary or revoke the Covenant. The approval holder must notify the department in writing within 24 hours of receiving any notice that the Covenant has been varied, revoked or otherwise altered or deemed invalid.
If the Positive Public Covenant is revoked, or if the Minister so directs following a variation, change or finding of invalidity, then the person taking the action must continue to manage the biodiversity offset area identified in Schedule 1 of this notice for conservation and they must enter into an alternate form of conservation covenant over the biodiversity offset area. The alternate form of conservation covenant must be agreed to by the Minister (noting preferred forms include conservation agreements made under s69B of the National Parks and Wildlife Act 1974 (NSW) and Trust Agreements made under s30 of the Nature Conservation Trust Act 2001 (NSW)).
The person taking the action must register the alternate conservation covenant on the land title of the Awaba Biodiversity Conservation Area within one year of any revocation, or of a direction by the Minister. Once the alternate covenant is registered, its provisions must be implemented.20/04/20151010.
