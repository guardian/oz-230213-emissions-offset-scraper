12. To offset residual impacts as a result of the temporary loss of approximately 234.8ha and the permanent loss of approximately 22.6ha of Greater Bilby habitat, the approval holder must:

Develop and implement a cat and fox control program. This program must:


be consistent with the Threat Abatement Plan for Predation by Feral Cats, 2008 and the Threat Abatement Plan for Predation by the European Red Fox, 2008.


cover an area defined by a 15km radius from the centre of the project site, as illustrated in Annexure 2, with accessibility determined by the presence of roads.


be undertaken with the involvement of appropriately skilled traditional owner(s).


consist of at least four cat and fox control campaigns per year for as long as mining activities occur within the project site.


not use poisoned baits (except within the landfill site(s)) to ensure that native species are not harmed.




Develop and implement a weed control program. This program must:


i. cover a corridor of 50m along roads used during the implementation of any mining activities within the project site and the access road from the project site to Tanami Road, including the junction of the access road and Tanami Road, as illustrated in Annexure 2.
ii. be undertaken with the involvement of appropriately skilled traditional owner(s).
iii. consist of at least four weed control campaigns per year for as long as mining activities occur within the project site or until the area described in b.i. is determined to be weed free by a suitably qualified person.
c.  Provide at least 20,000 per calendar year to Greater Bilby research for as long as mining activities occur within the project site. The research must:
i. focus on the Tanami Desert region and/or mining-related interactions.
